MIAMI 21 RULES

What the Miami 21 code allows.

Miami's zoning code — Miami 21 — permits ADUs on most T3-zoned (single-family) lots through Section 3.13. The framework is one of the most permissive in Florida:

  • Max ADU size: 800 sq ft, no more than 50% of the primary dwelling.
  • Setbacks: 5 ft side, 10 ft rear (subject to existing structure rules).
  • Height limit: 25 ft.
  • Owner-occupancy required: the property owner must live in either the main house or the ADU.
  • Parking: one off-street space for the ADU.
  • Short-term rental (under 30 days): not permitted in T3 zones for ADUs.
Miami-Dade unincorporated areas follow the County's separate ADU ordinance — similar in spirit but with different setback and lot-size requirements. If your address is in unincorporated Miami-Dade (not within a municipal limit), refer to Miami-Dade County housing.

PERMIT PROCESS

How to get an ADU permit in Miami.

  1. Pre-application zoning verification — confirm your lot is T3 or higher. Free, online via Miami's Planning Department portal.
  2. Submit building permit application — drawings, structural calcs, site plan. Tooly provides the structural and architectural package.
  3. Review and revision — typically 4–8 weeks of city review with comments.
  4. Permit issued — construction can begin.
  5. Inspections — foundation, framing, electrical, plumbing, final.
  6. Certificate of occupancy — you can move in or rent.

Total realistic timeline from contract to move-in: 15–22 weeks depending on city queue.
