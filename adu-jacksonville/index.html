JACKSONVILLE RULES

What Duval County allows.

The City of Jacksonville (consolidated with Duval County) permits ADUs in many residential zoning districts under its Planning and Development framework. Key parameters:

  • Max ADU size: 750 sq ft.
  • Setbacks: match underlying zoning — typically 7.5 ft side, 20 ft rear.
  • Height limit: 25 ft, one story.
  • Owner-occupancy: required in most districts.
  • Parking: one additional off-street space.
  • Short-term rentals: permitted in most areas with a license.
Beach municipalities (Atlantic Beach, Neptune Beach, Jacksonville Beach) have their own zoning codes separate from Jacksonville proper. Check the relevant city if your address is on the beaches.

Jacksonville rental economics

Furnished 1-bedroom rents in Jacksonville's core neighborhoods (Riverside, Avondale, Springfield, San Marco) sit at $1,150–$1,400/month. The beach communities and Mandarin push toward $1,500–$1,800. Lower entry rent than Miami or Tampa, offset by lower all-in cost.

PERMIT PROCESS

How to get an ADU permit in Jacksonville.

  1. Zoning verification — confirm ADU eligibility via the Jacksonville zoning portal.
  2. Building permit application — Tooly provides the architectural and structural package.
  3. Plan review — typically 3–5 weeks.
  4. Permit issuance — construction begins.
  5. Inspections + CO — standard FBC inspections.

Realistic timeline from contract to move-in: 12–17 weeks. Jacksonville moves notably faster than the South Florida cities on permits.
