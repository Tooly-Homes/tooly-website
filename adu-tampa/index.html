TAMPA RULES

What Tampa's ADU ordinance allows.

The City of Tampa Development Services permits ADUs in most residential zoning districts (RS-50, RS-60, RS-75, RS-100, and the RM districts). Highlights of the ordinance:

  • Max ADU size: 800 sq ft, capped at 60% of the primary dwelling's floor area.
  • Setbacks: 5 ft side, 10 ft rear (match the underlying zoning standard).
  • Height limit: 25 ft, one story typically required.
  • Owner-occupancy: not required. Tampa dropped this rule in 2022.
  • Parking: one off-street space; reduced if on a transit corridor.
  • Short-term rentals: permitted with a city license; some neighborhoods restrict.
Hillsborough County unincorporated areas have a separate ADU ordinance. Similar in spirit, slightly different setback rules. If your address is outside Tampa city limits, check Hillsborough Development Services.

Tampa rental economics

Furnished 1-bedroom rents in Tampa's core neighborhoods (Seminole Heights, Riverside Heights, Tampa Heights, V.M. Ybor) sit at $1,400–$1,700/month. South Tampa, Davis Islands, and Hyde Park push toward $1,800–$2,200. Cash-flow positive across the whole metro.

PERMIT PROCESS

How to get an ADU permit in Tampa.

  1. Zoning verification — confirm your lot allows an ADU. Check via the Tampa zoning portal.
  2. Building permit application — submit drawings, site plan, structural details. Tooly provides the package.
  3. Plan review — typically 3–6 weeks.
  4. Permit issuance — construction can begin.
  5. Inspections + CO — foundation, framing, electrical, plumbing, final.

Realistic total timeline from contract to move-in: 13–18 weeks. Tampa moves faster than Miami on permits in most cases.
